# Awesome Consumer Protection [![Awesome](https://awesome.re/badge-flat.svg)](https://awesome.re)

_A curated[^curation_details] list of awesome resources on consumer protection[^definition_consumer_protection]._

[^definition_consumer_protection]: (from Wikipedia, see citation at the bottom) **Consumer protection** is the practice of safeguarding buyers of goods and services, and the public, against unfair practices in the marketplace. Consumer protection measures are often established by law. Such laws are intended to prevent businesses from engaging in fraud or specified unfair practices in order to gain an advantage over competitors or to mislead consumers. They may also provide additional protection for the general public which may be impacted by a product (or its production) even when they are not the direct buyer or consumer of that product. CITATION: Wikipedia contributors. "Consumer protection." Wikipedia. Last modified January 15, 2026. Accessed February 2, 2026. <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consumer_protection>.

[^curation_details]: This list follows specific scoping guidelines. **Government Agencies** covers federal, state, and international consumer protection authorities. **Consumer Rights Organizations** features nonprofit advocacy groups. **Product Safety & Recalls** lists recall databases across agencies. **Financial Consumer Protection** includes financial complaint and counseling resources. **Complaint & Reporting Systems** covers fraud reporting platforms. **Legal Resources** includes attorney directories and legal aid. **Educational Resources** includes consumer guides and financial education tools. **Privacy & Data Protection** covers digital rights and identity theft resources. **Senior & Vulnerable Populations** lists resources for at-risk groups.

## Government Agencies

### United States Federal

1. [Federal Trade Commission (FTC)](https://www.ftc.gov): Protects consumers from unfair, deceptive, or fraudulent business practices.
2. [Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)](https://www.consumerfinance.gov): Enforces federal consumer financial laws and protects consumers in financial marketplace.
3. [Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC)](https://www.cpsc.gov): Protects public from unreasonable risks of injury from consumer products.
4. [Food and Drug Administration (FDA)](https://www.fda.gov): Regulates food, drugs, medical devices, and cosmetics safety.

### State & Local

1. [State Attorneys General - Consumer Protection](https://www.naag.org/issues/consumer-protection/): State-level consumer protection enforcement and mediation.
2. [State Consumer Protection Offices](https://www.usa.gov/state-consumer): Directory of state consumer protection agencies.

### International

1. [European Consumer Centre Network](https://ec.europa.eu/info/live-work-travel-eu/consumer-rights-and-complaints/resolve-your-consumer-complaint/european-consumer-centres-network-ecc-net_en): EU consumer rights assistance.
2. [Consumer Protection Australia](https://www.accc.gov.au/consumers): Australian Competition and Consumer Commission.
3. [Competition Bureau Canada](https://www.competitionbureau.gc.ca): Canadian consumer protection authority.

## Consumer Rights Organizations

1. [National Association of Consumer Advocates (NACA)](https://www.consumeradvocates.org): 1,500+ attorneys representing consumers victimized by fraud and abusive practices.
2. [Consumer Reports](https://www.consumerreports.org): Independent nonprofit providing product testing and consumer advocacy.
3. [Public Citizen](https://www.citizen.org): Consumer advocacy organization with focus on corporate accountability.
4. [National Consumers League](https://nclnet.org): America's pioneer consumer organization (founded 1899).
5. [Center for Auto Safety](https://www.autosafety.org): Independent, member-driven nonprofit advocating for auto safety and quality.
6. [Consumer Federation of America](https://consumerfed.org): Association of 250+ consumer organizations.
7. [Consumers International](https://www.consumersinternational.org): Global membership organization for consumer groups.

## Product Safety & Recalls

1. [SaferProducts.gov](https://www.saferproducts.gov): CPSC database for reporting and searching unsafe product reports.
2. [FDA Recalls, Market Withdrawals & Safety Alerts](https://www.fda.gov/safety/recalls-market-withdrawals-safety-alerts): FDA-regulated product recalls (food, drugs, medical devices, cosmetics).
3. [NHTSA Recalls Database](https://www.nhtsa.gov/recalls): Vehicle and equipment safety recalls (tires, car seats, etc.).
4. [USDA Food Safety Recalls](https://www.fsis.usda.gov/recalls): Meat, poultry, and egg product recalls.
5. [Sedgwick US Recall Index](https://www.sedgwick.com/product-recall/insights/us-index/): Quarterly reports on product recall trends.

## Financial Consumer Protection

1. [CFPB Submit a Complaint](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/complaint/): File complaints about financial products and services.
2. [CFPB Ask CFPB](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/ask-cfpb/): Database of answers to common financial questions.
3. [National Foundation for Credit Counseling (NFCC)](https://www.nfcc.org): Nonprofit financial counseling and education.
4. [Federal Student Aid Ombudsman](https://studentaid.gov/feedback-ombudsman/disputes/prepare): Student loan dispute resolution.
5. [SEC Investor.gov](https://www.investor.gov): Investment fraud reporting and investor education.

## Complaint & Reporting Systems

1. [FTC Complaint Assistant](https://reportfraud.ftc.gov): Report fraud, scams, and bad business practices to FTC.
2. [Consumer Sentinel Network](https://www.ftc.gov/enforcement/consumer-sentinel-network): Secure database of consumer complaints (for law enforcement).
3. [BBB Scam Tracker](https://www.bbb.org/scamtracker): Better Business Bureau scam reporting and tracking.
4. [IC3 (Internet Crime Complaint Center)](https://www.ic3.gov): FBI's cybercrime complaint system.
5. [Ripoff Report](https://www.ripoffreport.com): Consumer-driven platform for reporting business misconduct (controversial).

## Legal Resources

1. [NACA Attorney Directory](https://www.consumeradvocates.org/find-an-attorney): Find consumer protection attorneys by location and specialty.
2. [National Consumer Law Center (NCLC)](https://www.nclc.org): Nonprofit advocacy for consumer justice and economic security.
3. [Consumer Financial Protection Bureau - Legal Resources](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/practitioner-resources/): Resources for attorneys and legal aid providers.
4. [Legal Services Corporation](https://www.lsc.gov/what-legal-aid/find-legal-aid): Find free legal aid for low-income consumers.

## Educational Resources

1. [Consumer Action Handbook](https://www.usa.gov/consumer): Federal guide to consumer rights and complaint filing.
2. [FTC Consumer Information](https://consumer.ftc.gov): Articles on scams, identity theft, credit, and more.
3. [CFPB Financial Education](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/consumer-tools/): Tools, calculators, and guides for financial decisions.
4. [Scam Awareness Resources](https://www.consumer.ftc.gov/scam-alerts): Latest scam alerts and prevention tips.
5. [Consumer World](https://www.consumerworld.org): Catalog of 2,000+ consumer resources.

## Privacy & Data Protection

1. [Privacy Rights Clearinghouse](https://privacyrights.org): Nonprofit consumer information and advocacy on privacy.
2. [Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF)](https://www.eff.org): Digital rights and online privacy advocacy.
3. [Identity Theft Resource Center](https://www.idtheftcenter.org): Support for identity theft victims.
4. [Have I Been Pwned](https://haveibeenpwned.com): Check if email/data has been compromised in breaches.

## Senior & Vulnerable Populations

1. [AARP Fraud Watch Network](https://www.aarp.org/money/scams-fraud/): Scam alerts and resources for older adults.
2. [National Center on Elder Abuse](https://ncea.acl.gov): Resources on elder financial exploitation.
3. [Senior Medicare Patrol](https://www.smpresource.org): Medicare fraud prevention for seniors.
